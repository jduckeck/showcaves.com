Largest Cave Passage


A passage is a morphologic feature of a cave: a tunnel-like structure with about the same height and width for some elongation. At the first glimpse it seems to be a strange idea, that nature would produce such a special form of a hollow space. But as a matter of fact, passages are the common thing in caves, not special. This is a result of the way most caves are formed. Karst caves are often formed or at least altered by underground rivers. Those rivers work more or less the same way all along their underground bed. The result is a gorge-like passage with similar erosional forms as above ground gorges.

Actually it is impossible to make a ranking based on three dimensions. There may be one passage which is wider, another one which is higher, so which one is the biggest? And it gets more complicated if you add the length, is a long but small passage bigger than a huge and very short one? And how about changing dimensions, narrow in some parts and wide in others, do you count the maximum width althout it is found only once? Or do you take the width which is measured very often? Or the minimum width? Or try to compute the average width from all measurements? The result is in all cases rather inadequate and will change with every new survey. The solution could be to multiply all three values to get the volume. Unfortunately this number would be even more inaccurate as every cave passage has a rather complex form, widening and narrowing all the time. Current sureveying techniques are not able to measure the volume of a cave. As a result there is actually no way to compare passages unless one is bigger in all three dimensions.

The discussion on the "biggest cave" was actually started by National Geographic, who published an article subtitled "the biggest cave of the world" on Han Son Doong. They never explained how they did the comparison, even the dimensions were vague, estimated by the photographer or based on early raw survey data. The British team which explored the cave has published more accurate numbers after their second expedition which are less enthusiastic. With this in mind you should interpret the following list as a collection of extremely big caves without a strict ranking.


Height Width Length Name Description
180m 91m 3,000m Hang Son Doong The huge passage has a rather small entrance, but the passage behind has a general size of 80m high and 80m wide. There are two dolines and the passage is even bigger around those dolines, reaching a height of 200m and a width of 175m at the bottom. The huge main passage has a length of 3km, the survey length of the cave is some nine kilometer. The numbers are a bit confusing as various publications give different numbers.
120m 152m 1,931m Gua Payau (Deer Cave) Deer Cave is a huge passage, a through cave with the general dimension of 120m high and 100m wide. There is one part which is 168m wide and 125m high. Both portals are huge, more or less of the same size, but the bigger one is also said to be the biggest cave entrance of the world. Actually it is only number two in this respect, as far as we know. While it is generall wider and higher than Son Dong it is only 2km long.
146m 120m 2,600m Skocjanske Jame (Skocjan Caves) The Skocjanske Jame of the Classical Karst definitely plays in the same league, although it is probably not number three. And it is the best accessible of all huge passages, as it is well developed as a show cave and located in the heart of Europe. The Martel Chamber, which is not on the tour, is one of the largest underground chambers in Europe, 2.2 milllion m³, 146m in height, 120m wide and 300m long. And despite the name which says "chamber" ist is actually a part of the passage.
