Description

The Coaltown Museum at Westport is a mining museum dedicated to the local coal mining heritage. It includes what they call a true-to-life coalmine, a replica of a local colliery with machinery and tools. The mining technology is explained in a movie.

The most impressive exhibit is probably the 20 ton brake drum used on the Denniston incline. This steep hillside with an angle of 45Â° was used to transport the coal from the Banbury Mine near Denniston to the valley. The machinery was adapted to this incline and the brake was used to stop and hold a so-called Q wagon, a huge trolley full of coal. The Denniston incline is on the list of Historic Places and a DOC Site. It may be visited with the Banbury Mine Tour.

There are other exhibits on gold mining, saw milling, transport, maritime, a mineral collection, and a brewery. So it is actually a historic museum with a strong emphasis on mining.
