History

1997 Czech project Namak starts exploration.

Description

3N Cave became known worldwide, after the cavers of the Czech project Namak (which means salt in Persian) surveyed for 10 years and then published an article. The told in that article that it is the world's largest salt cave, after this had been a cave in Israel near the Dead Sea before for decades. It was true, sure, but it was a short fame, the Israeli intensified research and only a few years later the Sedom cave was again the longest. But several salt caves of Iran, especially Qeshm island, are now found in the top tem list. And after the cavers and the journalists came the tourists. Today there are numerous salt caves on Deshm and Hormuz island which are regularly show to tourists on guided cave trekking tours.

The actual name of the cave is, despite the fact that it is located on Qeshm island which belongs to Iran, in Czech. The cave was named Jeskyne Tri Nahacu (Cave of Three Naked Men) by the discoverers. The reason is simple, it is extremely warm inside, and cave exploration is hard work. And the cave is dry and "clean", there is no mud to get dirty. Add 2+2 yourself.

The idea to explore this cave was from the Czech Professor Pavel Bosak, the famous speleologist. He worked here in 1990, and he found many large salt caves and other karsts features and almost no literature.
