Ligne Maginot

Maginot Line


The Ligne Maginot (Maginot Line) was named after the French Minister of War André Maginot who planned the fortification. Built was actually only the part along the borders to Germany and Italy, the rest was skipped, and as a result it was a complete fail. In 1940 the German army occupied France in a few days by travelling through occupied Belgium and the Netherlands. The line became a metaphor for expensive efforts for a false sense of security. Merriam Webster defines the term Maginot Line as: "2: a defensive barrier or strategy that inspires a false sense of security".

The problem with border fortification is always the same, this applied for the Chinese Wall, the Roman Limes, and any later fortification. It is always only as strong as its weakest part. Once it is broken at one point the enemy will attack from behind, or simply ignore the fortification while conquering the country behind. The Maginot line was built between 1929 and 1938. Most forts were completed many years befor the war, and they were not manned. But when World War II started in 1939 the whole fortification was manned with army and reservists. The Maginot Line was invulnerable to aerial bombings and tank fire, had underground railways as a way to move men and material. It also had state-of-the-art living conditions for garrisoned troops, supplying central heating and eating areas for their comfort. It was electrified, the generators were needed for many purposes, including telefone, water pumps and air filters.

In other words, the Maginot Line was impervious to most forms of attack, and consequently the Germans invaded through the Netherlands in 1940. Finally, most fortifications surrendered after the armistice, which was signed on 22 June in Compiègne. They had their first military action in 1944, when the Allied forces invaded. Again most of the line was bypassed, but there were some heavy retreat battles near Metz and in northern Alsace. And again the Germans tried to regain some ground and started Operation Nordwind in January 1945. At this time, the Maginot Line casemates and fortifications were utilized by Allied forces. And so a part of the line was actually used for the purpose it had been designed for and showed what a well-designed fortification it was. On the other hand, by this time the German army was almost defeated, and this last attack was carried out by the Volkssturm, children and old men who had been forced into military service.
