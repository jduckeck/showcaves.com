History

1420 staircase dug by King Alfonso of Aragon's troops in a single night during the siege of the city.

Description

The Grotte du Puits Saint Barthélémy (Cave of the Saint Barthélémy Well) is located at the foot of the cliff on the southern side of the Bonifacio peninsula. It is reached on a trail which starts at the Escalier du roi d'Aragon and ends at the cave entrance. According to legend, the staircase was dug in 1420 by King Alfonso of Aragon's troops in a single night during the siege of the city. In reality, the stairs are older. It climbs down to sea level on 187 steps.
