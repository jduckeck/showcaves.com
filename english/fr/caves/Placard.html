History

1853 cave discovered.
1877-1878 destructive excavations in search of spectacular objects.
1902 important excavation financed by the association française pour l'avancement de sciences.
1912 findings analyzed by Abbe Breuil.
1958 excavations by Jean Roche.
1988 new discoveries by Louis Duport.

Description

The Grotte du Placard is an important archaeological site which was inhabited between 80.000 and 5.000 BC. The most famous excavator here was Abbe Breuil, who used the restults of the excavation of four layers of Magdalénian to establish a chronology in 1912. This work still serves as a reference today. The Magdalénian finds include bone spears, jewelry made of teeth and pierced shells, and so-called command sticks.

A more recent discovery by Louis Duport in 1988 is rather spectacular. He found a passage with engravings from the Solutrean period (~18.000 years BC). After the excavation of the passage the wall showed a 5 m long frieze of horses, deer, ibex, reindeer, a chamois, a saiga antelope, aurochs and two buffalos.
