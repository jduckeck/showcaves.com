History

1560 start of mning by the Ducs de Lorraine.
17th cty heyday of the mine.
1761 mine closed.

Geology

The crystalline basement of which the Alsace mountain ridge consists is fractured and has numerous lodes, in this case rich in copper ore.


Description

Les Hautes-Mynes (the High Mines) are located high up in the upper Moselle valley, in the Vosges. This mine was operated by the Ducs de Lorraine and was very modern, as the miners very early used black powder to create the tunnels.

The visit includes the museum called la Maison des Hautes-Mynes, the surface exhibition of mining machinery at the mine St Thomas and the mine St Charles with a total of 60m underground passages. The second part of the visit is the optional underground visit to the mine de la Rouge-Montagne (Mine of the Red Mountain). For the underground tour of 180m the visitors are equipped with helmets and headlamps.
