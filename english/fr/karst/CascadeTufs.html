History


Description

The small river Cuisance springs from the debris at the end of the Cirque du Fer à Cheval south of Les Planches Près Arbois. This is a karst spring, most likely a collapsed entrance of a river cave similar to nearby Grotte Les Planches. The spring forms a shallow pond and the the Cuisance flows down the valley. The limestone rich water reaching the surface air looses carbon dioxide, more carbon dioxide is consumed by lichen, moss, and other plants in the river. The water is not able to keep the limestone dissolved and it precipitates. The deposition of limestone is disturbed by the movement of the water and the plants, which are encrusted. The plants die and decompose, leaving holes in the limestone. The result is tufa, a porous limestone which forms dams in and around the river. The river changes its bed and so it deposits a plateau with a steep escarpment at he end where the river forms a waterfall.
