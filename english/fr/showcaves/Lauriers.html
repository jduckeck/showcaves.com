History

1930 discovered by Martel and de Joly.
1946 explored.
1989 Claude Chantemesse and Patrick Martin, the owners of the cave, developed and opened it to tourists.
1991 show cave opened to the public.
01-JAN-1998 Société Touristique Aven Grotte des Lauriers creatend.
2001 cave closed.
17-JAN-2003 Société Touristique Aven Grotte des Lauriers liquidated.
2016 Upper entrance to the Lauriers system closed by the municipality of Laroque for protection.

Description

The Grotte des Lauriers is located in the Gorges de l'Hérault, the gorge of the river Hérault. The cave was discovered by E. A. Martel and Robert de Joly in 1930. Visited and explored by numerous speleologists, four or five cave entrances with different names were found to be connected. It was finally developed as a show cave by Claude Chantemesse and Patrick Martin, the owners of the cave. They used the Grotte du Lion which is the cave entrance which is located at the Chemin de la Draille. So it is a rather new show cave, opened only three decades ago, and also a short-lived one, as it was closed after a few years. It attracted only 12,000 visitors per year, probably a result of the nearby Grotte des Demoiselles which was far more famous. But the main economic problem was the lack of a car park, so the visitors had to be transported to the cave entrance from some distance with a shuttle. The cost of this shuttle made the cave unprofitable. After the death of the owner the cave was closed after 20 years of operation.

The Grotte du Lion entrance is located strategically at the Hérault river, which was the only way to reach the hinterland. That was probably a reason for the people to stop here. The cave was inhabited between 20,000 and 15,000 years BP, excavations revealed thousands of objects from this time. Later the cave was located at the single lane gravel road through the gorge called Chemin de la Draille. But the current road is the two lane D986 which was constructed lower. The old road is not open, though it might be used for an easy walk to the cave both from the lower turnoff or from the upper. Nevertheless, we recommend the Parking escalade Thaurac, which is right below the cave entrance. It is intended as a viewpoint of the river and the gorge, but there is also the trailhead of a steep trail to the cave entrance.

Today the location is mainly known among climbers. The cliffs around the cave entrance offer numerous routes. The cave itself is sometimes also known as L'Aven des Lauriers, because it has several vertical shafts. As far as we understand there are now cave trekking tours after appointment which are more on the climbing side. They include the introduction into abseiling and single rope technique. So they are intended for people with some physical fitness. Full caving gear including climbing gear is required. The entrance section is a crawl, followed by an abseil into a passage with numerous speleothems. The tours use the show cave part, there are still trails and rusty iron railings, but no electric light. The cave trekking tours are organized for groups by the nearby village Laroque.
