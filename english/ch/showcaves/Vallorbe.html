History

1962 cave discovered by cave divers behind the spring.
07-APR-1974 opened to the public.
1987 second part of exploitation.

Description

The entrance of the Grottes de Vallorbe is close to the source of the Orbe, a very nice karst spring. In 1962 divers tried to dive the siphon of this spring and discovered the cave behind it. It took 12 years until it was opened to the public, the cave is now accessible by an 80 m long artificial tunnel. The cave is a long and winding tunnel leading to the subterranean river Orbe. It contains beautiful speleothems like straws and calcite crystals.

The valley does not end at the cave and resurgence. The source of the Orbe is at the hamlet Les Rousses, near the town Les Rousses, more than 30 km southwest at 1070 m asl. After 20 km the river flows into Lac de Joux, which is connected to Lac de Brenet at the village Le Pont. The level of the lakes is at a height of 1,000 m asl. The lakes have no outflow, the water sinks into swallow holes under the lake and after flowing through the cave reappears at a height of 820 m asl, 2.5 km to the northeast. The river Orbe gave its name to the valley (Val d'Orbe) and the town Vallorbe, which is just a short form of Val d'Orbe.

The legend of Donat
In ancient times, according to local legends, the Grotte aux Fées was inhabited by fairies, hence the name. A young and handsome blacksmith from Vallorbe by the name of Donat ventured into the cave to check whether this was really true. But tired of his walk, he fell asleep on a bed of ferns in front of the cave. When he woke up, he found a fairy who asked him to live with her inside the cave. Donat was offered a gold coin and a pearl a day and access to knowledge for a century. However, the fairy forbade him to see her in her chambers.
But he was curious, and a few days later, when she was sleeping, he into her chamber and noticed her feet were shaped like a crow's foot. The fairy woke up, went mad, and chased him out of the cave. She warned him, not to reveal anything of what he had seen. But Donat told the whole story when he returned. As a result his gold coins turned into dead leaves, and disappointed he left the country and was never seen again.
