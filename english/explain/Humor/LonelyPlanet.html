Lonely Planet


The Lonely Planet is a well-known travel guide that has been around for many decades. It comes from Australia, so it is in English, and its blue covers are typical. We can definitely recommend it as a travel guide. Of course, caves and exhibition mines are also described, and as sometimes happens, you occasionally find amusing errors. This page serves to collect them.


  • On the page for the Cheung Po Tsai Cave:

    This "cave" – in reality little more than a hole in some rocks – ...

    Uh, yes, exactly: that is the definition of the term cave: an underground hole large enough for people to enter.
  • On the page The most important travel tips summarised:

    >And it’s not just mosquito bites and ticks you should protect yourself against when travelling – in Transylvania, it’s best to use plenty of garlic to ward off vampire bites too.
