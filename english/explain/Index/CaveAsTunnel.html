Caves Used as Road Tunnels


A rather rare thing is a natural cave used to build a road or railroad tracks through. To do this the cave has to have two entrances, be big enough and the location has to be somewhere, where a road is needed. It is easy to understand that this happens quite seldom.

Caves of this type have an enormous benefit: you can visit it by driving through! On the other hand, it is rather hard to see much of the cave if you drive through with maybe 80 km/h.

There are at the moment four caves with a road built through, and one with a railroad. The road cave at Domusnovas, Sardinia, Italy, has been closed for the traffic.

  • Grand Arch, Jenolan Caves, Australia.
  • Grotte du Mas-d'Azil, France.
  • Grotta di S. Giovanni di Domusnovas, Italy.
  • Natural Tunnel, U.S.A..
  • La Cuevona, Spain.
