Cave Churches


Caves were not only a place to live, they always were a place to worship god, gods or whatever. The Mithras cult of ancient Rome was using solely caves for their divine service. So there was only one step from using a cave to the integration of a church in a cave.

Here are some examples of cave churches. Most of them are in Europe.

  • Barranco de Guayadeque, Spain.
  • Village troglodyte Rochemenier, France.
  • Loire Troglodytes, France.
  • St Ninian's Cave, Britain.
  • Agia Sophia, Greece.
  • San Giovanni d'Antro, Italy.
  • Peştera Ialomita, Romania.
  • Sveta Jama, Slovenija.
  • Grotto of St. Peter in Antioch, Turkey.
  • Cave Dwellings of Cappadocia, Turkey.
  • Pesterna Crkva Sveti Erazmo, Macedonia.
  • Pesterna Crkva Sveti Stefan, Macedonia.
  • Pesterna Crkva Sveti Arhangel Mihail, Macedonia.
  • Pesterna Crkva Sveta Bogorodica, Macedonia.
  • Pesterna Crkva Sveti Atanasie, Macedonia.
  • Muqattam Mountain Cave Cathedral, Egypt.
