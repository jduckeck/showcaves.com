Troglomorphy

Adaption to the Cave Environment


Troglomorphy is the process of getting adapted to the special conditions of caves. The animals do this by

  • Loss of pigmentation:
    pigmentation is necessary as a protection against sunlight, especially the ultraviolett wavelengths. In caves this protection is obsolete.
  • Loss of eyes and learning to navigate blind:
    In caves the possibility to find ones way is esential. Possible alternatives are sound, tactile and smell.
