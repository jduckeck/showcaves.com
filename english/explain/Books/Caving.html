Vertival Caving

Vertical caving is a more sportive version of caving. Every cave tour will include several vertical parts, so the vertical techniques are useful for every caver.

  • Allen Padgett, Bruce Smith (1987): On Rope, National Speleological Society, 1st edition (December 1987), hardcover.
    The ultimate book about vertical caving. The new edition is in print.
    at Amazon.com
  • Alan Warild (1994): Vertical, National Speleological Society, 3rd edition (June 1994), paperback.
    at Amazon.com
    online version at CaveDiggers.com

Surveying

An important part of cave exploring is the surveying of caves. It is neceassary to document what was found. And it is an area where modern techniques can be used very effective.

  • George R. Dasher (1997): On Station, National Speleological Society; ISBN: 1879961032; (January 1997),* Hardcover: 242 pages
    at Amazon.com
  • B. Ellis (1988): Introduction to Cave Surveying, National Speleological Society, (June 1988), paperback.
    at Amazon.com
  • Kenneth C. Thomson, Robert L. Taylor (1991): The Art of Cave Mapping, National Speleological Society, loosele edition (December 1991), spiral-bound.
    at Amazon.com
