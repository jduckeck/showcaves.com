History

1928 opened for the public.

Description

The Allander Tropfsteinhöhle (Dripstone cave of Alland), named after the nearby town Alland, is located at the slopes of the Großer Buchberg (467m asl) in the Wienerwald. The cave is rather small, but it is one of the largest caves in Lower Austria. It consists of rifts and bedding plane passages, some up to 12m high.

The Allander Tropfsteinhöhle, as the name suggests, is noted for its nice speleothems. The cave shows stalacties, stalagmites and moonmilk. It is also a paleontological site, with remains of brown bears (Ursus arctos). A 10,000 years old brown bear skeleton is on display in the cave in a small exhibition.
