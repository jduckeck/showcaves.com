History

13th century created by the noble lords of Rettenberg-Hoheneck.
1265 Possessed by Conradus de Foramine, servant of the Hoheneggers.
1315 definitely owned by Tyrol, new construction or building activities are recorded.
1328 Vainly besieged, presumably by Hohenegger.
14th century abandoned.
1546 stored 394 falcon balls in the ruins, in connection with the Schmalkalden campaign.
1552 Falkon balls recovered and transported to Ehrenberg fortress.

Description

The castle Burg Loch can be reached by a short steep climb. It consists of a front wall with an integrated residential building. The castle and its foundations were renovated some time ago.
