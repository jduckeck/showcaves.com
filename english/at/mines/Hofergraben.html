Description

The Schaubergwerk Hofergraben or Hofer-Graben is located in the Grünalmtal. The area was inhabited by the Slavic peoples during the 6th and 7th century, and they called the mountain slope Rudnig (ore mountain). So they knew already about the local ores. But mining started actually in the 15th century and was abandoned in the 17th century. There is a ore body of about 4m, mainly copper but also some gold. The ruins of the ore processing can be seen opposite the entrance. The ore was of poor quality and so the mining was not very rentable.

The mine is reached on a 30 minutes walk from the village Hopfgarten into the gorge Hofergraben, after which it was named. The mine has electric light and is easily visitable on self guided tours. The floor is covered with at least 10cm of water, but the trail is elevated on wooden planks. Nevertheless, good shoes are advisable.

The mine is mentioned on many pages, but there is no current information. There is some chance it is closed now. Please send us an email if you know more.
