History

22-APR-1925 developed as a show cave and opened to the public.
14-JUN-1949 declared a Natural Monument with the number ZI. 4292/49.

Description

This place is actually called Einödhöhlen (Einöd Caves) because there are two caves, one named Einödhöhle, the other named Elfenhöhle (Elves Cave. Einöde is the German word for wasteland, a strange name as it is located in the beautiful Wienerwald, the hillcountry in which the Alps end south of Vienna. Probably the surroundings were not very well suited for agriculture, and so the small village, which today belongs to Pfaffstätten was named Einöde. The caves were named after the nearby village. They are located above the small town at the slopes of the Pfaffstättner Kogel.

This small cave is exceptional, as it is said to be a sea cave, not a karst cave. It was formed by the waves of the Tortonisches Meer (tortonic sea) and the Pontischer See (pontic lake) during the upper Triassic. At this time the plains which are now the fertile Wiener Becken and Pannonic Plain were covered by water, first a lake with sweet water, later a sea with salt water. The waves of this lake were forming erosional planes and cliff lines with their the mechanical force.
