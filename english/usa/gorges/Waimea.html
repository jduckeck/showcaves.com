Description

Waimea Canyon is not only the biggest canyon on Hawai'i, it is also the Grand Canyon of the Pacific. Waimea is the Hawaiian word for reddish water, a reference to the colour of the Waimea River caused by the erosion of the canyon walls. There is a town at the coastal road with this name, and two roads starting to the north The lower road goes into the canyon along the river, some people are actually living here, but the road ends after only 3km. Of course you can walk from here. The other road is the rim road, Hawaiʻi state road 550, running along the western rim of the canyon, with numerous outlooks into the canyon. The last outlook at the end of the road is directed to the north and the Na Pali coast. Here you can see the green mountains from "Jurassic Park".

From the outlook befor, the last outlook into the canyon, you can see the upper end of the canyon. And on the other side is a very flat hill, some would say its a plain. This is Mount Waiʻaleʻale, the summit of the island Kaua'i. It is also the wettest point on Earth. One of them actually, as there are several which claim to be the wettest, and all are right. But the important point is: do not walk there, unless you have the best rain protection equiment which exists, or you simply dont care. Fortunately on this side of the canyon the weather is much better. So the main consequence is for photographers: do the trip in the afternoon. The road is on the western side and the clouds are on the eastern side. If you want sunshine in the canyon you have to wait for afternoon.

The Waimea Canyon is a result of erosion, but the reason why it is so big is tectonic. When the island Kaua'i was formed, the volcano erupted and produced layer above layer of basaltic lava flows. More than 5 Million years ago the island reached the surface and continued growing. But 4 Million years ago the island became unstable and collapsed. The western part broke off and slid down a fault line. This created a huge depression at the western slopes of the volcano which then filled with lava flows.
