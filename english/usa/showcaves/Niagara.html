Description

Niagara Cave is a limestone cave with some formations and nice river cave profiles. It has a small river which forms waterfalls, the largest being 18m high. This waterfall is called Niagara, after the Niagara Falls at the Canadian border, and is the reason why the cave is called Niagara Cave. The limestone contains several fossils, like Sunflower Coral. One room of the cave is used as a wedding chapel, where more than 300 weddings took place.

The cave was discovered in 1924, reportedly by a farmer who sent his children down a sinkhole to look for three little pigs who had been falling down into the sinkhole. According to legend the kids climbed down, discovered the cave, rescued the pigs and clikmbed back to the surface. We have no idea if it is true, but it is a pretty common discovery story.
