History


Description

Gruta do Lapão (Lapão Cave) is a huge cave passage and a gorge. The site is called the Reserva Ambiental Gruta do Lapão or ECO - Fazenda Gruta do Lapão, and is a private nature reserve. This is called Reserva Particular do Patrimônio Natural (RPPN) or Private Natural Heritage Reserve in Brasil. The owner of the land, Max do Carmo, has a sort of youth hostel accommodation for groups and offers trips into the huge cave on the property. The cave is visited on cave trekking tours, which also include abseiling into the gorge. He is obviously a caver and has the necessary knowledge, and he actually hosted a caving conference a few years ago. All necessary equipment is provided, which includes helmet, headlamp, and climbing gear.

The cave is named after the Serra do Lapão, the mountain ridge along the coast. The mountain ridge consists mostly of insoluble rocks and thus has no other caves, this is the only cave in the area located in a small karstified patch of limestone. The visit includes the main chamber named Cathedral, which is 120 m long, 20 m wide, and 21 m high. The cave is a gorge-like passage, a through-cave which is 480 m long.
