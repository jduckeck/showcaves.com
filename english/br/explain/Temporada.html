High and Low Season

Alta Temporada - Baixa Temporada


In tourism there are two seasons, high and low season. Actually those seasons, unlike for example summer and winter, depend on the number of people which travel. So if there are many visitors/travelers its high season. As a result there might be numerous high seasons during the year. Many operators have different times and/or fees during high and low season. Unfortunately tourists are often not aware when those actually are. As a rule of thumbs this are school holidays and different Holidays and festivities, which might be a weekend or even a full week.

During Alta Temporada (High Season) the fee is higher, probably longer open hours too, as during Baixa Temporada (Low Season). In Brazil, the high season is in the months of December, January and July.

  • South Region: in this region, the low season takes place in the summer. This is because the mountains of Rio Grande do Sul and Santa Catarina receive a greater number of visitors in winter, when there is a chance to see the snow.
  • North Region: in this region, the seasons are divided between the flood period (from February to July) and the dry season (from August to January). As most tours are made on rivers, the season of greatest demand is in the dry season.
  • Midwest region: this region receives many tourists during the school holidays.
  • Southeast region: this region receives tourists all year round, high season are school holidays, carnival, and religious festivals.
