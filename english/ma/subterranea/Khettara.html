History


Description

A Khettara is a form of irrigation system developed in the 15th century. The water was transported through underground canals from the aquifer to the farms for irrigation. The construction of these khettara was a communal task, they were built and maintained by the whole village. Khettara is the name in Morocco, in Iran they are called qanats and in Algeria foggaras.
