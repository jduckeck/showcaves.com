History

1992 survey by Laos expedition.

Description

Tam En (Cave of Swallows) was traditionally used by locals to cross the mountain.

In March 1993, Princess Maha Chakri Sirinthorn of Tailand, also named princess Phra Tep, King Rama IX’s daughter, went to Takhek and wished to visit sight. She was led to Tam En and was enthusiastic about the cave and its further development. This was a great incentive for the local authorities to develop the cave as a show cave. The cave got concrete trails, stairs, and coloured light. In front of the cave a garden park was installed. The cave river Te was dammed to create a long lake which allows boat trips. Today it is major tourist place in Khammouane, with thousands and thousands of visitors per year.
