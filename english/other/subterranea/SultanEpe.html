History

1982 necropolis declared a local historic monument.

Description

Султан-эпе (Sultan Epe) or also Сұлтан Үпіһ (Sultan Üpi, Sulatn Upi) is located close to Sultan Epe Necropolis, both important archaeological and cultural sites. There are currently archaeological excavations. This is an underground mosque which was named after the Saint Sultan-Epe. It was dug into limetone and has nine chambers.

Sultan-Epe was the son of the famous Central Asian mystic poet Khakim-Ata (Suleiman Bakyrgani), a disciple of Ahmad Yassawi and Ambar-Ana.
