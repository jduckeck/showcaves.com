1961 2 km of passages explored and surveyed.
1964 4 km of passages explored and surveyed.
04-DEC-1984 Cave is closed.
JUL-1997 16 persons wird der Rückweg vom Wasser abgeschnitten, sie könne die Höhle jedoch nach Rückgang des Wassers unverletzt verlassen.

Description

The Salzgrabenhöhle (Salzgraben Cave) is closed and can only be entered with permission from the Berchtesgaden National Park Administration. Moreover, it is a rather difficult cave that requires sound experience, both in mountaineering and climbing, as well as in speleology. We list such caves on showcaves.com only in rare exceptional cases, usually to warn. Freely according to the motto, nothing is so bad that it cannot still serve as a cautionary example.

Although it is not open to the public, it became one of the most famous caves in Germany in the 1980s. One reason for this was that it was the longest and deepest cave in Germany for quite some time. In addition, it has an extraordinary amount of climbing by German standards, which made it extremely popular with speleologists and extreme sports enthusiasts. Even today, one reads reports which portray the cave as an underground via ferrata. This led to a dramatic increase in visitor numbers in the 1980s. There were more and more cave accidents, but also massive pollution of the cave. In 1984, the Berchtesgaden National Park Authority drew consequences and closed the cave. Anyone wishing to visit it must prove qualification and sign a liability waiver.


This cave is not a show cave!
It may be visited only with the necessary training, equippment, and preparation!
