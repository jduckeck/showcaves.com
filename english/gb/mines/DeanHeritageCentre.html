History

1979 Dean Heritage Museum Trust formed to protect the heritage of the Forest of Dean.
1981 site at Camp Mill acquired.
1983 Dean Heritage Centre opened to the public.

Geology


Description

The Dean Heritage Centre is an open air museum about history, culture, and art of the Forest of Dean. The museum has an exhibition about the local mining history with numerous exhibits including rails, wheels and mileposts for horse-drawn tramways, and the Lightmoor beam engine. There is an Iron Forge which was in operation between 1612 and 1674. A number of such forges were built at this time by order of King James I, who controlled the mining rights in the Royal Forest of Dean. During this time the Forest of Dean was the main iron-producing area in England. The Iron Foundry was set up by Samuel Hewlett in 1823. The 19th Century was a time of great expansion for the industries in the area. The foundry was in operation until 1876.
