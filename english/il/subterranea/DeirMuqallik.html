History

5th century monastery established by Euthymius and Theoctistus.
NOV-1873 visit of the site by Conder and Kitchener.

Description

Deir Muqallik is a monastery and cave church complex, founded by St. Teoctistus. It is located in a wadi which is known as Nahal Og, Wadi Og, Wadi al-Muqallek, or Wadi Muqallik. The wadi drains the winter rains of the West Bank, from the eastern slopes of the Mount of Olives to the Dead Sea.

The ruins of the Monastery of Theoctistus are located halfway down the dry valley. They are located high in the steep northern walls, and rather difficult to access. The monastery was established by Euthymius and Theoctistus in the 5th century. This age is mentioned in Byzantine sources and archaeological evidence confirms that the main phase of the cave church complex was in the 5th to 7th century. At the end of the 8th the monastery was deserted.
