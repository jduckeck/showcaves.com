History


Description

The Museo di Speleologia Luigi Fantini (Luigi Fantini Speleology Museum, MuS) is a speleological exhibition at the University of Bologna, which was created by the speleological group GSB-USB. It is intended as a point of reference for the conservation of speleological equipment used from the 1930s to today. The exhibition includes helmets, suits, belts, carbide cylinders, relief materials, and lighting systems. Many of those objects are unique, as they are self-built or modified for the purpose by generations of speleologists. In addition to the exhibition part there is an educational laboratory.

The MuS lent its historical caving equipment to the movie Il Buco by director Michelangelo Frammartino. It tells the exploration of the Bifurto abyss in Calabria in 1961. The movie was shown on the 78th Venice Film Festival.

The museum started as a mineralogical collection of the USB. a result of speleological activities between 1957 and 1970. In 1970 the Italian Speleology Society promoted a vast campaign for a drastic reduction in sampling, even for scientific use. This mineral collection was displayed in the second floor of the Cassero di Porta Lame. The director of the Museum is Paolo Forti, is Nevio Preti.

The Porta delle Lame or simply Porta Lame was a gate of the outer medieval walls of the city of Bologna. The gate was built together with the medieval walls in 1334, and had two drawbridges, one for carriages, and the other for pedestrians. It was rebuilt in 1677 in a Baroque style by the architect Agostino Barelli. The most recent restoration was between 2007 and 2009. The wall has been demolished, but the gate remains and is today located in the center of a roundabout on the Piazza VII November 1944 at the end of Via Lame, where it meets via Zanardi. The entrance to the musuem is inside the gate.
