Description

The Dolina Pozzatina (Pozzatina doline) is one of the largest dolines in Italy. The steep walls and the flat bottom are covered by soil, which has been transported inside by water. As a result it contains a rich flora and fauna. Depending on the direction and the amount of sun it gets, the bottom and the walls are coverd by shrubs of termofile evergreen species such as holm oaks, alaterno, lentisk. But there are also species which require more humid climates, such as Turkey oak, ash-tree, maple, and elm. Amongst the fauna are large mammals, like foxes and badgers, predatory birds like buzzards and kestrals, oriols, jays and many sparrows.

The doline has been formed by the collapse of a chamber of a karst cave and the continuous erosion caused by water flowing into the pit. Originally vertical walls became steep slopes, which are now covered by forest.

This doline is not signposted, and it is a little tricky to find it. So we try to describe it a little bit more detailed. The doline is located only a few dozen meters from the road SP48 Sannicandro Garganico to San Marco in Lamis. It is about 7km from the turnoff near Sannicandro Garganico. The doline is close enough to be seen, or at least guessed from the road. There is a (pretty poor) gravel road turning off north of the doline, and after about 200m a field of white limestones with karren on the left side is the place to stop. From here its only a short walk through shrubs to a nice lookout point.
