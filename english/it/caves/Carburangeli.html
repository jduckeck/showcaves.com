    History

     
    1865 first archaeological excavation by G.G. Gemmellaro.
    1996 Nature reserve founded.

    Description

    The Grotta di Carburangeli is said to be one of the most important caves of Sicily. It is a nice karst cave with speleothems but also an important archaeological site. The first excavation was made by G.G. Gemmellaro in 1865 and revealed terracotta sherds from the bronze age. Palaeontological findings include bear, small elephants, wolf, horse, and ox.
