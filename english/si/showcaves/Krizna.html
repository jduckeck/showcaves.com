History

1832 first documented.
1878 Ferdinand von Rochstetter, member of the Vienna Academy of Science, and Ferdinand Schulz, Curator of the Ljubljana Museum, excavated over 2000 cave bear bones in only four days.
1926 first exploration of the water filled parts by Slovene cavers.

Description

Krizna Jama is the subterranean bed of a river and is famous for its 22 lakes. Also the remains of more than 100 cave bears were found in a side passage near the entrance. Two reassembled skelettons are shown in the Vienna Natural Science Museum, simply because they were excavated at the end of the 19th century by Austrian Professors, when Solvenia still belonged to the Austro-Hungarian-Empire.

Krizna Jama has a quite large portal at the end of a short gorge. This portal is closed by an iron bar gate to protect the cave, mainly the bear bones and the bats living in the cave. Inside the portal, after a descent to the level of the main passage, the passage widens and becomes quite big. The passage is between 15 and 25 m high and wide. The floor is a natural cave floor with clay and rocks, including huge blocks from the ceiling. There is a rather basic trail, but it has some stone steps, and so there is no need to climb, and due to the size no stooping. Speleothems are not very common, but when there are stalactites they are quite large and ímpressive. Stalagmites, on the other hand, are extremely rare and unimpressive, mostly because of the cave river which frequently floods the floor and destroys them. Only where the water does not reach, the stalagmites are of normal size.

Visits are only possible in times of normal water levels. When there is too little water, the boat rides do not work, when there is too much water, the path might be flooded, and it becomes dangerous to visit. Fortunately, both situations are quite rare. All the necessary equipment for viewing the cave's 22 lakes is made available by the guides. For the first part, no special equipment is needed. It is possible to tour only this part, called tourist trip or short trip. It still includes a short boat ride at the far end. For the wet tour rubber boots, caving suits, battery lights and inflatable boats are essential. This tour shows 13 lakes and ends at the calvary, a huge pile of debris covered by stalagmites.
