Description

Tham Pla is a river cave, famous for the fish living in the water called Pla Mung or Pla Khang. They are of the same family as carps, named Soro Brook Carp (Pla Mung). The fish are believed to belong to the gods, so they are not caught by the locals. They become up to one meter long. Visitors can feed the fish from wooden platforms, if they buy the fruit and vegetable bags at the entrance. A 450m long path leads from the park entrance, across a suspension bridge, and then to the cave.

Tham Pla is a popular attraction. It is part of Tham Pla Pha Suea National Park. It has several brooks and cool hilly forests which are used for relaxation. Namtok Pha Suea is a waterfall in Tambon Mokchampae about 26km from the provincial seat on Route 1095 to Pai district.
