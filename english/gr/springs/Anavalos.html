Description

Πηγές Αναβάλου (Anavalos Spring) is a submarine fresh water spring, which is used for water supply. A water-filled cave ends a few meters below the sea level at the foot of a limestone cliff. Unfortunately, this means the sweet water mixes with salt water and is not suitable anymore. The salt water even enters the cave and inside the cave is a halocline, during winter with higher production the salt and sweet water are mixed by the current and become brackish. Such a submarine spring is rarely usable for drinking water, and a lot of valuable sweet water is lost.

The ancient name of the spring is Δίνη (Dini), that's how it was named by Παυσανία (Pausanias) 2500 years ago. According to Pausanias, the ancient Argives threw horses decorated with bridles into the spring to appease Poseidon. The name Dini is still used in some publications, but the modern name is Ανάβαλος (Anavalos).

The spring was cased to seal out the undesired salt water and provide drinking water for the village. A semicircular dam was built, which was possible because there was a rocky ledge in front of the cave opening. On the northern end is the building with the pumps. The dam and the building were erected in the 1960s, when many submarine springs at the Mediterranean were cased. However, it seems the water quality

The area of Arcadia is very dry during summer. The mountain ridges are not fertile, mostly they are bare karst with many limestone rocks and little soil. But the river valleys have a flat floor and are suitable for agriculture. Unfortunately the profitable agricultural crops require irrigation during summer, so the area is always in need of sweet water. Lately an irrigation project of the agricultural lands of the Municipality of North Kynouria with the water of the spring was started. The construction of the necessary water pipes will cost about 6.1 Million Euro.

On the southern end of the dam is a small church named Εκκλησάκι Αγίου Γεωργίου (Ekklisia Agios Georgios, church of Saint George).
