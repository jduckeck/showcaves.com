History

8th century BC described by Homer.
5th century BC described by Herodotus.
late 4th century BC building erected.
167 BC burned down by the Romans.
18th century monastery of St. John the Baptist built on top.
1958-1964 excavated by the archaeologist Prof. Sotirios Dakaris.
1976-1977 more excavations by Sotirios Dakaris.

Description

The Νεκρομαντεῖον (Nekromanteion of Ephyra) is the only oracle of the death in Greece. The word Nekromanteion means "oracle of the death", and the people came here to talk with their late ancestors. It belonged to the Thesprotians, an early Greek tribe who settled in this area about 2000 B.C. It was even mentioned by Homer, who obviously knew the place. In the Odyssee he sent Odysseus to seek advice from a long-dead oracle, and he was horrified by visions of Hades.
