History

1947 Welkom founded following a major gold discovery.

Description

St. Helena Gold Mine is a working gold mine located at Welkom, the state capital. Welkom was planned completely, before the first brick was laid. The architect was Sir Ernest Oppenheimer who then was Anglo-American Chairman. The result is called Garden City, where mining and industrial areas blend harmoniously with the rest of the city.

In the Welkom Library is the Gold Museum, which is an exhibition about the local gold mining. It is possible to visit the mine and its wine cellar at 857 m below the surface, which is the worlds' deepest wine cellar.

There are actually three tourist spots in the town which are frequently listed, the underground mine tours, the underground wine cellar and the gold museum. Unfortunately, there is not a single line of actual information on the web, they are only mentioned by websites citing other websites. Not even the tourist information page of Welkom lists further details. There is no info on tourist portals like tripadvisor. There is no local tourist information, and the governmental tourist organization has only dead links. The online impression of this state is that tourism was shut down completely. While this is most likely not the case, it seems to us that all three tourist sites of Welkom are actually closed, which is quite sad.

There are still six operating gold mines in and around Welkom. All of these mines offer guided tours of the mine site and also underground. The guided tours are usually only organized on weekdays, although the tours take place on different days and at different times. It is therefore best to enquire at the local tourist office in Welkom town hall.
