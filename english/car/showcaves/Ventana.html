Description

Cuva Ventana translates Window Cave, the name is obviously with the picture from the entrance portal. The cave entrance is located high above the valley of the Rio Grande de Arecibo. The outlook on the valley is breathtaking, probably the best part of the caves. The caves ticket office is located at the Carretera 10, behind a gas station named Any Time Bosque Bello. The tours are on the full hour, but you should arrive at least half an hour earlier, to get helmet, lamp and safety instruction. And to sign the liability waiver. Also there is a walk to the cave, which can only be reached on foot. Be sure to wear appropriate walking shoes.

The caves have trails, mostly stamped earth, railings, but no electric light. The guides are said to be trained biologists, because of the multitude of cave animals. There are insects, snakes, and bats. Actually there are two caves, Cueva Ventana and Cueva . The Cueva is a small through cave, which is crossed on the way to Cueva Ventana.

The cave is long known to locals and was visited more or less without permission by the owner. In 2009 scenes for the Disney movie Wizards of Waverly Place: The Movie were filmed in the cave. The popularity increased and finally the owner of the ground who is also the owner of the gas station introduced daily fees in 2013. Security measures like wooden boardwalks and a parking lot behind the gas station were financed with the fee. For some reason there were different fees for different days of the week, probably because Saturday and Sunday are more popular.

The helmet provided by the operator is a simple one, intended for construction workers. It has no chin straps abd no headlamp, the lamp provided is handheld. If you have a real helmet with a headlamp we suggest to bring your own. You will have your hands free for other things. Probably the reason why they provide handheld lamps: it makes it difficult to touch the formations.
