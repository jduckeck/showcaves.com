Pelagonian Massif


The Pelagonian Massif (PM) consists mostly of Precambrian formations, including highly metamorphosed crystalline rocks. The Proterozoic complex is a relict of the Grenville continental crust with a typical internal structural construction of domes. However, there is a marble formation which is up to 3 km thick and allows the formation of caves. The caves are found in the northern part and along the western border.
