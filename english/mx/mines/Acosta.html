History

1727 mine opened.
1985 mine closed.
1993 show mine opened to the public.

Description

The Mina de Acosta is located at the northeastern city limits of Mineral del Monte. The abandoned mine is operated as a show mine by the Museo de Minería de Pachuca. The guided tour includes a historic introduction, the house of the superintendente de la mina (Chief of the mine), and a 450 m long underground tour. The underground tour shows the technology of mining from the 18th to the mid 20th century.

Many immigrants, especially the miners, were from Cornwall, so the mining is strongly influenced by Cornish mining. The tour also explains the Cornish heritage of the area, for example, the popular soccer which was introduced by them.
