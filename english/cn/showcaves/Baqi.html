Description

八奇洞 (Bā qí dòng, Baqi Cave) is a "scenic area", the Chines euphemism for "theme park". It offers the only 410 m underground high-speed slide in the world and the world’s first underground cave wall cinema using the 100 m cave wall as a screen. Located only 25 km west of Beijing city center, it is easily reached by millions of people and thus quite popular. But the interesting thing is that the cave, which is part of the theme park, is definitely quite impressive. It is not only quite huge, it also contains (probably) the biggest cave chamber in a show cave in China, which is 200 m long, 130 m wide and 110 m high. The 100 m high vertical wall was used to install a ‘wall cinema’, the wall is used as a screen.

There are always weird world record statements given by cave administration and guides in China. Fact is that speleology worldwide discovers new caves, and extends the knowledge about existing, as a result such superlatives change frequently. Another problem with large chambers is that the ranking is difficult, see Biggest Underground Chambers for more details.

The cave is located in a temple area, and the grounds are full of temple buildings and Buddha statues. But some of them actually look more like modern art than actual religious symbols, for example a doorway which looks the negative of buddha cut into a rock slab. However, the actual Tanzong Temple is located 1.3 km to the north. Buddhism was first introduced to Yancheng in the Jin Dynasty. At this time, famous Buddhist were teaching in this area, and it soon became a center of Buddhism.

Like always the description of this cave is quite ridiculous. The cave is famous for its unique folds of "8" and eight wonders, so it is known as "the wonder of heaven and earth, the wonder of the cave"! And its one of the rare caves which have a constant temperatures. Really? The passages are huge but mostly devoid of speleothems. The artificial tunnel into the cave is a long staircase, and they built a slide into the tunnel, so it’s possible to slide down instead of walking.
