Description

张家界玉皇洞 (Zhangjiajie Yuhuang Cave) is, despite the name, not a cave but a series of cave temples. The 300 m long cave passage contains Shallow grottos and overhanging cliffs were used in the 19th century to place sculptures inside, and were thus transformed into cave temples. There are actually eight caves with the names Fengquan Cave, Yujin Cave, Haobi Cave, Mochi Cave, Hulong Cave, Xiongshi Cave, Kongsheng Cave, and Yinguo Cave. Yuhuang Cave is the most spectacular because of a 10 m high statue of the Jade Emperor. The Yuhuang Grottoes were commisioned by Li Jingkai, a man of local fame during the period of Emperor Jiaqing during the late Qing Dynasty (1368-1911). He employed numerous local stone artists between 1799 and 1807
