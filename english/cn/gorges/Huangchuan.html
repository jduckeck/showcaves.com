Description

湟川三峡 (Huáng chuān sānxiá, Huangchuan Three Gorges) is the gorge of the Huangchuan river, which is a major tributary of Beijiang River. The river springs at Hongyan Mountain in Xingzi, hence the old name, today it is called Lianjiang River. It has a total length of 180 km, flows from north to south through Lianzhou, Yangshan and Yingde, and finally flows into the Beijiang River.

The underground river flows through four karst towers in a gorge like passage with a length of 1,500 m. The gorge has 18 bends and has three sections with names, 龙门峡 (Lóngmén xiá, Dragon Gorge), 莲花峡 (liánhuā xiá, Lotus Gorge) and 香蕉峡 (xiāngjiāo xiá, Banana Gorge). Thats why it is called .
