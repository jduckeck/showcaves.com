History

1954 discovered by mining.
1972 opened for the public.
1995 enrolled into UNESCO's World Heritage List.

Description

Ochtinska Aragonitova Jaskyna is, as the name says, an aragonite cave, the only one open to the public in Slovakia. The Aragonite occurs in kidney-shaped, needle-shaped and spiral forms.

The geological situation is really interesting and unique. In the Ochtinsky cryptokarst Palaeozoic limestones are located between phyllites, which are non-soluble rocks. In the Upper Cretaceous, part of the limestones was hydrothermically transformed into ankerites and siderites.

The rich aragonite filling of the cave was formed under the specific conditions of this geological situation.
