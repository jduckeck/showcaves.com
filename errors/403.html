You may have misspelled the URL

If you have typed in the URL manually, please check first, if you have misspelled it. Be carefully with capital letters, they make a difference!

The link/bookmark you followed is outdated

We sometimes move pages, when we add new sites or sections. This applies for the small countries, if the number of entries for a country increases, and we decide to make a separate chapter for this country. It also happens when the cave is not unique, and we must adapt the filename to have two caves with the same name. This is actually more frequent that you may guess. But the shortest way to a page will be the navigation bar at the bottom. The search works only with names, not fulltext, but you will find any page on showcaves.com.

Tell us about broken links...

  • If you followed a link from a foreign page, please tell us where the broken link was (the page you where coming from). Just paste its URL/title into an email and send it to E-mail: . We will do the best to give the author the new address of the page.
  • If you found the link in a search engine, there is nothing we can do about it. But search engines update their links continually and chance is, that it will be updated in a few days or weeks.
  • If you think the page was deleted, you may check the Wayback Machine, see link below. They scan our site and have older versions. However, if we delete a page, there is a good reason. Caves which are just closed to the public are not deleted. Sometimes we started a page but never found enough info to fill it, so we delete the empty page after some time. Or we delete it because the site is listed twice, that’s rare but actually happens
