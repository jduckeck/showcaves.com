Page Deleted

The page you were looking for does not exist any more, it was deleted. This is extremely rare, as we do not delete pages when a site is closed to the public. Our policy is to provide the same info and add a warning, so that people do not go there just to find it closed. If pages are renamed because the name of the site changed, we add a redirect, which will bring you to the new page. But sometimes we created a page, typically with little content, because we did not find out much. And in the end it was misinformation and the site never existed. Sometimes we started a page but never found enough info to fill it, so we delete the empty page after some time.


Such empty or obsolete pages are deleted, and you followed a link directly to such a page and ended here.

Please Tell us about broken links...

We do not log your request, so we do not know about the fact that you found this page, a result of the GDPR. Your privacy is protected. We would therefore ask you to send us a quick message so that we can resolve the issue.

  • If you followed a link from a foreign page, please tell us where the broken link was (the page you where coming from). Just paste its URL/title into an email and send it to E-mail: . We will ask the author to remove it.
  • If you found the link in a search engine, there is nothing we can do about it. But search engines update their links continually, and it will be removed in a few days or weeks.
  • You may check the Wayback Machine, to see the page how it once was. However, if we delete a page, there is a good reason. Sites which are just closed to the public are not deleted.
